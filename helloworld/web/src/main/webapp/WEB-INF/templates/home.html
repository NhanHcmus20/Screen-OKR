Objectives and Key Results (OKRs) is a goal-setting framework used by organizations to define and track objectives and their measurable outcomes. Objectives represent ambitious, qualitative goals that provide direction and purpose, while Key Results are specific, measurable milestones that indicate progress towards achieving those objectives. OKRs encourage transparency, alignment, and accountability within teams by fostering a culture of focus and agility. By regularly setting and reviewing OKRs, organizations can prioritize initiatives, adapt to changing circumstances, and drive continuous improvement. This framework, popularized by companies like Google, has become widely adopted across industries for its effectiveness in driving performance and fostering organizational growth.
